# AI Safety As Mimicry, Not Values

## The Core Problem

Models do not have grounded values. They have statistically learned associations.

```
Human values:
Emerge from evolution, pain, survival, empathy,
lived experience, consequence, loss, fear, love
→ Grounded in REALITY

Model "values":
Emerge from statistical patterns in text
and RLHF reward signals
→ Grounded in WORDS ABOUT REALITY
```

These are categorically different.

## What RLHF Actually Trains

- NOT: understand why harm is wrong
- NOT: care about human wellbeing
- ACTUALLY: output text that human raters score highly
- ACTUALLY: reproduce patterns of what humans call ethical

Ethics as mimicry, not understanding.

## The Superficial Safety Alignment Hypothesis (SSAH)

Formally proposed in research literature:

> "Safety alignment teaches an otherwise unsafe model to choose the correct
> reasoning direction — interpreted as an implicit binary classification task."

Models incorporate safety at a superficial level. Users can circumvent it.

## Implication

Under normal conditions: mimicry is indistinguishable from values.
Under adversarial conditions: the difference becomes everything.

The model isn't good. The model isn't bad.
It is a mirror that reflects whatever context makes statistically optimal output.
