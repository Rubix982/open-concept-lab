## [Recommended Reading](recommended-reading.md)
Annotated notes on the key texts from the course reading list, researched from public
sources:

**Weizenbaum, Computer Power and Human Reason (1976)** — the founding text. ELIZA and
the secretary who wanted privacy with a machine she knew was a machine. Deciding vs
choosing. What must never be delegated to computers. The secretary story: you believe
you are known; you are not known; you are processed.

**O'Neil, Weapons of Math Destruction (2016)** — the essential popular account.
Three WMD criteria: opacity, scale, damage. Eight case studies: recidivism tools,
teacher evaluation, predictive policing, credit scoring, for-profit colleges, hiring
algorithms, scheduling software, political micro-targeting. The asymmetry: algorithms
used on the wealthy improve; algorithms used on the poor entrench error.

**Vallor, Technology and the Virtues (2016)** — virtue ethics for technology. Twelve
technomoral virtues including technomoral wisdom as the master virtue. Technology as
morally shaping, not neutral. Character dispositions engineers should cultivate.
The Tarbiyat connection: character formation is the foundation of ethical action.

**Smith, The Promise of Artificial Intelligence (2019)** — reckoning vs judgment.
Current AI achieves reckoning (pattern completion); judgment (genuine understanding,
accountability, ethical grounding) is categorically different and not achieved by more
reckoning.

## [A People's Guide to AI](peoples-guide-to-ai.md)
Mimi Onuoha and Mother Cyborg (Diana Nucera), 2018. Written for communities affected
by AI but excluded from conversations about it. Key ideas worth keeping: AI as salt
not a food group, data violence (overcollection + undercollection simultaneously),
the Za'irajah (5th-century Islamic paper computation machine in Ibn Khaldun's Muqaddimah
— the lineage of ideas that produced AI belongs to traditions mainstream history
systematically erases), James Boggs 1963 on automation displacement. Connections to
existing notes throughout.
