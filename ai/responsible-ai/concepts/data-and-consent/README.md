# Data and Consent

Data permission, litigation, researcher outreach, and the cultural dataset business.
The through-line: the honest path was always available. It was slower. It was chosen
against. The costs are now arriving.

---

## [Data Permission and Litigation](data-permission-and-litigation.md)
The permission question that was never asked. Two paths: Path A (MIT is MIT, standing
permission, honest) vs Path B (scrape first, construct legal theory afterward, what
actually happened). The litigation landscape: NYT v. OpenAI, Authors Guild, Getty Images,
GitHub Copilot class action, music industry. The tobacco delay strategy: delay the reckoning
long enough to maximise extraction. The training data provenance trap: capability was built
on contested foundations. The Spotify moment: what forced negotiation at scale might look like.

## [Data Intimacy and Ethics Limits](data-intimacy-and-ethics-limits.md)
What "intimate data" actually means: what you search at 2am, what content makes you stop
scrolling, what you type and delete. The incentive misalignment: the more intimate the data,
the more precise the targeting, the more valuable the platform — the incentive structure
actively rewards extraction. Incidental harm vs structural harm: embedded ethics cannot
reach systems whose design goal is the harm. The biomedical comparison — why Canca rejects
the gatekeeping model, and where that rejection has its own limits.

## [Consent and Researcher Outreach](consent-and-researcher-outreach.md)
The three-tier data strategy for the knowledge infrastructure: Tier 1 (arXiv, OpenAlex,
MIT-licensed — fully open, build here first), Tier 2 (paywalled but navigable via researcher
contact), Tier 3 (exclude without explicit consent). The mirror effect: contacting researchers
gives them something nobody else gives them — how their work is understood and what it
connects to. The email template. Expected response pattern from 100 emails. Non-profit
structure rationale. This is simultaneously ethical consent, Wizard of Oz UX testing,
and community building.

## [Cultural Dataset Business Idea](cultural-dataset-business-idea.md)
A critical niche: building consented, culturally diverse datasets for AI training — solving
the dataset geography problem (Western-centric training data that erases non-Western
intellectual traditions). The moat: relationships, not data. Any company can scrape. No
company can replicate years of trust-based partnerships at speed. The race logic that
dominates AI development is the enemy of the trust this requires. Four-layer model:
dataset licensing, audit/certification, custom construction, non-profit for sensitive
communities. Per-culture framework table. The Bau Lab as proof of concept and pilot.
