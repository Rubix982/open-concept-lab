# Political Economy

The structural causes underneath AI ethics failures. Neoliberalism as the operating
system, regulation as a contested terrain, and the consistent pattern that the people
making decisions about AI are not the people who will live with the consequences.

Almost nothing in AI governance is fully explicable without these notes.

---

## [Neoliberalism as Operating System](neoliberalism-as-operating-system.md)
The load-bearing note for understanding why AI governance fails structurally. Neoliberalism
is not one issue among many — it is the operating system most AI governance problems run on.
The efficiency norm as invisible normative default. The winner-takes-all endgame as a
self-defeating strategy. Documented history: Powell Memo (1971), Chicago School, Thatcher/
Reagan, Washington Consensus. The China threat narrative as manufactured urgency (BYD/Tesla
contradiction). The "difficult but not impossible" regulatory momentum problem and the
chokepoint chain where momentum dies.

## [Ethics as Defanging](ethics-as-defanging.md)
How "AI ethics" framing neutralises accountability. Five mechanisms: infinite deferral,
abstraction of real harm, ethics teams as cover, managing serious people into sophisticated
harmlessness, eliminating consequences from vocabulary. Think tank parallel — manufactured
consensus, Overton Window, distraction function. The honest vocabulary: rights, law,
liability, representation. Structural interventions. Taleb's skin in the game standard
with biographical context. What will not work.

## [Technology and Authoritarianism](technology-and-authoritarianism.md)
Langdon Winner's "Do Artifacts Have Politics?" applied to AI. Nuclear technology required
authoritarian social conditions; the same argument for AI. The democratic dilemma: moving
at democratic speed means losing the AI race; moving at AI-race speed means suspending
democratic processes. China case: speed is real, hidden costs are real, borne by those
who could not say no. The incremental erosion risk — how democracies become less democratic
through accumulated emergency exceptions.

## [AI Regulation Ecosystem and Pace](ai-regulation-ecosystem-and-pace.md)
Five-layer ecosystem (compute, datacentres, data, models, agents) with specific regulatory
questions per layer. The "is the world going to die without AI?" argument. Slowing down
as a values choice not a technical necessity. Growth rate vs wellbeing — the evidence from
slower-growth, higher-wellbeing societies. Security carve-out. The neoliberal context:
who pays when we don't slow down?

## [Environmental Cost of AI](environmental-cost-of-ai.md)
The physical reality of "the cloud." Polluter pays principle applied to datacentres.
Korangi (Karachi) industrial zone as the externality made maximally concrete — same
mechanism as AI datacentres, different scale. The water bill gap: delivery price vs
resource value. Integrated levy-to-infrastructure model with desalination funding
mechanism. Sequential build argument and the prisoner's dilemma of competitive
infrastructure deployment.

## [Goodhart's Law and the Prestige Trap](goodharts-law-and-the-prestige-trap.md)
When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. The FANG arc: early 2010s
genuine novelty vs 2026 process-driven maintenance. The CS credential bubble: 30-40 to 445
students in 27 years, signalling value collapse, cost borne by candidates. The Shirky
Principle. Goodhart applied to every AI hiring metric. What actually compounds genuine
cross-disciplinary thinking vs what the filter finds.

## [Rawls and Distributive Justice](rawls-and-distributive-justice.md)
John Rawls, A Theory of Justice (1971). The veil of ignorance thought experiment. Two
principles: equal basic liberties and the Difference Principle (inequalities only just if
they benefit the least advantaged). The maximin rule. Applied to AI distribution systems:
credit scoring, hiring, healthcare, criminal justice as Rawlsian mechanisms. The veil of
ignorance test for algorithms: would you design this if you didn't know which side of the
decision you'd be on? The rizq connection — personal accountability as distribution node.

## [Human Safety in AI Landscape](human-safety-in-ai-landscape.md)
Comprehensive research note on three dimensions of human safety in AI. Psychological:
Wysa (FDA designation, RCTs), Limbic (NHS scale), Character.AI Setzer case (first
wrongful death settlement), MIT SERC addiction study. Physical: Waymo vs Cruise (dragging
incident and shutdown), Tesla Autopilot 29 fatalities, IBM Watson dangerous oncology
recommendations, Viz.ai genuine narrow-task safety. Occupational: Meta $52M PTSD
settlement, Amazon warehouse injury rates, Workday liability precedent (first AI tool
vendor directly liable for employment discrimination), Scale AI DOL investigation.

## [AI on Social Media and Children](ai-on-social-media-and-children.md)
Snapchat's My AI as the clearest case of structural harm: the harm is the mechanism, not
a side effect. The philosopher analogy — that person would be in prison. Generational
damage evidence: internal Facebook research, 2012 mental health inflection. Why parental
controls fail. Structural fixes: prohibition of AI companions for minors, liability not
fines. Broader case for banning AI from social media.

## [Categorical Data Exclusion](categorical-data-exclusion.md)
Products in harm-recognised categories (tobacco, gambling, addictive social media)
should only collect minimum necessary data for minimum necessary purpose — as an
absolute with criminal enforcement, not a guideline. Age verification without identity.
No opt-in pathway for broader collection. Why the regulatory judgment is already made
and the intervention that follows from it has not been made. The gap is political,
not philosophical.
